BB84 Protocol - Prepare & Measure QKD

Bennett & Brassard (1984) | Implementation: BB84Protocol Class

~5%
Typical QBER
QuNetSim
Framework Used
  • Quantum Transmission: Alice prepares qubits in random rectilinear (+) or diagonal (√ó) bases
  • Random Measurement: Bob measures in randomly chosen basis without prior knowledge
  • Basis Reconciliation: Classical channel comparison reveals matching bases
  • Key Sifting: Discard mismatched bases, retain ~50% of transmitted bits
  • QBER Estimation: Sample subset to calculate quantum bit error rate
  • Error Correction: CASCADE algorithm corrects bit flips from channel noise
  • Privacy Amplification: Universal hashing compresses key against eavesdropping
Implementation Features: Realistic channel noise modeling (~5% error rate) | Key rate calculation based on processing time | Comprehensive post-processing pipeline

E91 Protocol - Entanglement-Based QKD

Ekert (1991) | Implementation: E91Protocol Class

~3%
Lower QBER vs BB84
EPR Pairs
Entangled Qubits
  • EPR Pair Generation: Quantum source creates entangled Bell states distributed to Alice & Bob
  • Random Basis Selection: Independent random choice of measurement bases (XY, XZ, YZ)
  • Simultaneous Measurement: Both parties measure their entangled qubits
  • Basis Reconciliation: Classical communication reveals matching measurement bases
  • Bell Inequality Test: CHSH inequality validation confirms quantum entanglement
  • Key Extraction: Exploit quantum correlations from matching bases for key bits
  • Post-Processing: Error correction and privacy amplification for final secure key
Key Advantages: Device-independent security | Lower QBER (~3%) | Bell state validation | Quantum correlation-based key generation
